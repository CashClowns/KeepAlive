How to Keep Microsoft Teams Status Green in 2026

If you work remotely or in a hybrid environment, you already know the frustration: you step away from your desk for five minutes, come back, and Teams has flipped your status to Away. Your manager sees it. Your colleagues see it. And now you have to explain why you weren't "active."

The good news is there's a simple fix — and it doesn't require touching your computer's settings, asking IT for help, or buying any hardware.

Why Does Teams Show You as Away?

Microsoft Teams detects idle time by monitoring mouse and keyboard activity. By default, Teams changes your status to Away after approximately 5 minutes of inactivity. If your computer goes into sleep mode or the screen locks, Teams will also show you as Away or Offline regardless of your status setting.

This behavior is built into Teams and Windows itself — it's not something you can permanently disable from the Teams settings alone.

Method 2 — Set Teams Status Manually

You can manually set your Teams status to Available, which overrides the automatic idle detection for a limited time:

  1. Click your profile picture in Teams (top right)
  2. Click your current status
  3. Select Available
  4. Optionally select "Duration" to keep it active for a set time

The downside: Teams will still eventually switch you to Away if your computer goes idle, and you have to remember to reset it every time. It's a temporary fix, not a permanent solution.

Method 3 — Change Windows Power Settings

You can adjust Windows sleep and screen timeout settings to extend idle time:

  1. Open Settings → System → Power & Sleep
  2. Set Screen to "Never" and Sleep to "Never"

The problem: on corporate-managed laptops, IT departments often lock these settings so you can't change them. Ca$hClowns works around this without needing admin rights.

Method 4 — Use the Teams Focus Mode (Not Recommended)

Teams has a Focus Mode in Viva Insights that silences notifications but doesn't actually prevent your status from going Away. Despite what some articles claim, Focus Mode is not a solution for keeping your status green during inactivity.
